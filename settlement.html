Settlement Services

The Chinese Association of Mississauga helps new immigrants and seniors with important documents and settlement services. Our staff can assist you every step of the way through one-on-one or group consultations.

PR Card Renewal (枫叶卡更新)

A PR card is your proof of permanent residency in Canada. You need to renew it when it expires. We can help you with this process.

⚠️ Important: You must apply online. We can help you with this.

Who Can Apply?

You can renew your PR card if:

  • Your current PR card has expired OR will expire in the next 9 months
  • Your card is lost, damaged, or your name has changed
  • You have lived in Canada for at least 2 years (730 days) in the last 5 years

Required Documents

Prepare these items before you apply:

Mandatory Documents
  • Current PR Card
  • Landing paper
  • Passport or travel document valid within 5 years
  • Photocopy of all pages of the passport valid within 5 years
  • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • Two photos taken within 1 year (size 50mm x 70mm)
  • Address records in Canada and other countries within 5 years
  • Education records within 5 years (date, school name, city, province)
  • Employment records within 5 years (date, company name, city, province)
  • For underage applicants (under 18): birth certificate, school attendance record and transcript
  • All legal documents if official name was changed
  • Record of leaving and returning to Canada within 5 years (includes city and country while outside Canada)
  • If you left Canada for over 1095 days but stayed with a Canadian citizen spouse: spouse's Citizenship certificate, marriage certificate, and employment information
  • Application fee: CAD $50

How to Apply (5 Simple Steps)

  1. Get all your documents ready (see Step 2)
  2. Create an account on the Government of Canada's Permanent Residence Portal
  3. Fill out the online form (we can help you with this)
  4. Upload your documents and pay the $50 fee online
  5. Submit your application and keep the confirmation number

You cannot send a paper application by mail. It must be done online.

All information can be found on the IRCC website under:
"Immigration and citizenship" → "New immigrants" → "Get a permanent resident card" → "Apply for, renew or replace a PR card"

If you find any mistakes here, please refer to the IRCC website for accurate information.

After You Apply

Once you submit your application:

  • You will get a confirmation number to check your status online
  • Processing takes several weeks (check current times on government website)
  • Your new card will be mailed to your Canadian address
  • Do not travel outside Canada while waiting for your new card

Traveling While Waiting?

If you must travel outside Canada before getting your new PR card:

  • You need to apply for a PR Travel Document (PRTD)
  • Apply at a Canadian embassy, high commission, or consulate
  • This takes 4-6 weeks and costs extra money

We recommend waiting until you receive your new PR card before traveling.

Residency Requirements

How Long Must I Live in Canada?

To keep your permanent resident status, you must live in Canada for at least 730 days (2 years) during the past 5 years. This includes days before you became a permanent resident.

What Counts as Living in Canada?

Days you are physically in Canada count toward your 730-day requirement. Time spent outside Canada may count if:

  • You are accompanying a Canadian citizen spouse or partner
  • You are working for a Canadian company outside Canada

Special Situations

If you haven't lived in Canada for 2 years, you may still qualify if you have special reasons, such as:

  • Serious health problems
  • Family emergencies
  • Other exceptional circumstances

Citizenship Application (入籍申请)

We provide assistance with Canadian citizenship applications, helping you navigate the process from document preparation to submission.

Eligibility Requirements

To apply for Canadian citizenship, you must:

  • Be a permanent resident of Canada
  • Have lived in Canada for at least 1,095 days out of the past 5 years
  • Have filed your taxes (if required) for at least 3 years out of the past 5
  • Be able to speak and understand English or French
  • Pass a citizenship test (for applicants aged 18-54)
  • Not have any prohibitions (criminal convictions, etc.)

Required Documents

Prepare these items before you apply:

Mandatory Documents
  • Landing Paper & PR Card
  • Two pieces of government ID (such as SIN card, OHIP card, Driver's Licence, etc.)
  • Passport valid within 5 years (color photocopy required for commissioner certified true copy)
  • Previous citizenship applied date (if applicable)
  • Residential address within 5 years (date, address)
  • Record of leaving & returning to Canada within 5 years (date, country/city, reason for leaving)
  • Record of employment OR/AND education within 5 years (documents required, such as letter of employment, school transcript)
  • For child applications: Birth Certificate, and date of parents' Canadian citizenship if applicable
  • Two photos for citizenship application
  • English language ability proof for applicants aged 18 – 54 (such as LINC LV 4 and up certificate, CELPIP test certificate, transcript of local high school/university)
  • Any 3 years' Notice of Assessment within 5 years (all 5 years recommended)
  • If you stayed in one country over 183 days within last 4 years: local criminal check record
  • Application fee: adult – CAD $630, child (under 18) – CAD $100

Application Process

  1. Gather all required documents (see Step 2)
  2. Complete the application form (we can assist with this)
  3. Pay the application fee
  4. Submit your application online or by mail
  5. Take the citizenship test (if between 18-54 years old)
  6. Attend a citizenship ceremony

All information can be found on the IRCC website under:
"Immigration and citizenship" → "Citizenship" → "Apply for citizenship"

If you find any mistakes here, please refer to the IRCC website for accurate information.

Other Settlement Services

In-Home Visit Service 上门服务

If you are not able to come to the office due to transportation, handicap, or other issues, CAM is now providing in-home visit service.

Please contact our office first, we will determine if you need in-home visit service, and arrange an appointment for our social worker to visit you.

Low Income Energy Assistance 低收入电费补助申请

Eligibility:

  • You must be the utility account holder
  • Your AFTER TAX income must meet the following amounts:
    • 1 person household: $28,000 or less
    • 2 people household: $39,000 or less
    • 3 – 4 people household: $48,000 or less
    • 5 + people household: $52,000 or less
  • Anyone living in the house over age 18 must be included
  • Everyone included in the household must have filed income tax for the previous year

Documents required:

  • Utility bill
  • Driver's license of the utility bill account holder
  • Birthdates and names of all household members
  • Social Insurance Number (SIN) of all household members
  • Income Statement (Notice of Assessment) of everyone included in the household
